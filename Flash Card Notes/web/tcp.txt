1. Explain TCP, and why is it important.

  - TCP- Transmission Control Protocol - made of layers ( Application , Transport, Internet, Network)
  - Manages the sending and receiving of all data as packets
  - TCP checks if all packets are received
  - guaranteed mail service

2. What is TCP? When is it used? How does it work?

  - TCP is Transmission Control Protocol.
  - It is used every time you send or receive data.
  - Every time you receive packets, TCP checks the data.


3. What are the benefits of UDP over TCP? What are the shortcomings?

  - UDP has small packet sizes, connectionless, and more control when data is sent out.
  - UDP does not compensate for lost packets.
  - will not try to recover from corrupted segments
